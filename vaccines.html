Essential Childhood Vaccinations: Complete Guide

The BabyCare system provides automated notifications and comprehensive tracking for eight essential childhood vaccines. Each vaccine is carefully scheduled based on your child's age and medical guidelines to provide optimal protection during critical developmental periods.

Essential Vaccines For Newborn Children

Hepatitis B

Protects against Hepatitis B virus, given at birth and later doses.

Birth (0 months)
Detailed Information

The Hepatitis B vaccine is the first vaccine your baby will receive, typically given within 24 hours of birth. This vaccine protects against Hepatitis B virus infection, which can cause chronic liver disease, cirrhosis, and liver cancer.

Common Side Effects:

Mild fever, soreness at injection site

Schedule: Given at birth, 1-2 months, and 6-18 months

Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis (DTaP)

Prevents diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis. Administered in multiple doses.

2 months
Detailed Information

DTaP is a combination vaccine that protects against three serious diseases: diphtheria (affects breathing), tetanus (causes muscle spasms), and pertussis (whooping cough, especially dangerous for infants).

Common Side Effects:

Swelling, redness, mild fever

Schedule: Given at 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, 15-18 months, and 4-6 years

Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib)

Protects against Hib infections, which can cause meningitis and pneumonia.

2 months
Detailed Information

Hib vaccine prevents serious infections caused by Haemophilus influenzae type b bacteria, including meningitis, pneumonia, and severe throat swelling that can block the airway.

Common Side Effects:

Fever, irritability, swelling at injection site

Schedule: Given at 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, and 12-15 months

Pneumococcal

Prevents pneumococcal diseases like pneumonia, meningitis, and sepsis.

2 months
Detailed Information

The pneumococcal vaccine (PCV13) protects against 13 types of pneumococcal bacteria that cause serious infections like pneumonia, meningitis, and bloodstream infections.

Common Side Effects:

Mild fever, irritability, redness at injection site

Schedule: Given at 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, and 12-15 months

Polio

Protects against poliovirus, preventing paralysis and neurological complications.

2 months
Detailed Information

The inactivated polio vaccine (IPV) protects against poliovirus, which can cause paralysis, breathing problems, and death. Polio has been eliminated from most of the world thanks to vaccination.

Common Side Effects:

Redness at injection site, mild fever

Schedule: Given at 2 months, 4 months, 6-18 months, and 4-6 years

Rotavirus

Oral vaccine preventing rotavirus infections, which cause severe diarrhea.

2 months
Detailed Information

Rotavirus vaccine is given by mouth (oral) and protects against rotavirus, the most common cause of severe diarrhea and dehydration in infants and young children worldwide.

Common Side Effects:

Mild diarrhea, irritability

Schedule: Given at 2 months, 4 months, and 6 months (oral vaccine)
